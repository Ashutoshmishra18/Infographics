From theoretical foundations to the generative AI revolution — key moments in 75 years of artificial intelligence.

research & development
1950
The Turing Test
Alan Turing proposes a test of machine intelligence: if a human can't distinguish a machine from a human in conversation, the machine can be said to "think."
Research
1956
The birth of AI as a field
The Dartmouth Conference officially coins the term "Artificial Intelligence" — researchers believe human-level AI is a decade away.
Research
1997
Deep Blue defeats Kasparov
IBM's chess computer beats the world champion Garry Kasparov, marking the first time a machine defeated a reigning world chess champion under standard match conditions.
Breakthrough
2012
Deep learning revolution
AlexNet wins the ImageNet competition by a huge margin, sparking a deep learning renaissance. GPU-accelerated neural networks become the dominant AI paradigm.
Breakthrough
2017
"Attention Is All You Need"
Google researchers introduce the Transformer architecture — the foundation for every major language model today, including GPT, BERT, and Claude.
Architecture
2022
Generative AI goes mainstream
ChatGPT reaches 100 million users in 2 months — the fastest-growing consumer app ever — kicking off the generative AI era for the general public.
Product
2024–
Agentic AI emerges
AI systems can now plan, use tools, browse the web, write and run code, and work on multi-step tasks with minimal human supervision — entering the agentic era.
